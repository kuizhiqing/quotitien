Adagrad

It adapts the learning rate to the parameters, performing larger updates for infrequent and smaller updates for frequent parameters. For this reason, it is well-suited for dealing with sparse data. xk+1​=xk​−Gk​+ϵ​λ​∇f(xk​) Application : learned to recognize cats in Youtube videos; GloVe word embeddings.

Adadelta

Adadelta is an extension of Adagrad that seeks to reduce its aggressive, monotonically decreasing learning rate. Instead of accumulating all past squared gradients, Adadelta restricts the window of accumulated past gradients to some fixed size w.

Principal Component Analysis

In order to maximize variance, the first weight vector w1​ thus has to satisfy w1​=∥w∥=1argmax​{i∑​(x(i)​⋅w)2}=∥w∥=1argmax​{∥Xw∥2}=∥w∥=1argmax​{wTXTXw} Since w1​ has been defined to be a unit vector, it equivalently also satisfies w1​=argmax{wTwwTXTXw​} The quantity to be maximised can be recognised as a Rayleigh quotient. A standard result for a positive semidefinite matrix such as XTX is that the quotient's maximum possible value is the largest eigenvalue of the matrix, which occurs when w is the corresponding eigenvector.

Let x∈Rn be origin samples, projection vector w satisfies wTw=1. Denote Σ=N1​(X−Xˉ)(X−Xˉ)T the variance of origin samples, variance of projected samples, σ(X,w)=N1​(wTX−wTXˉ)(wTX−wTXˉ)T=wTΣw

Lagrangian multiplier form, maxwTΣw+λ(1−wTw), leads to Σw=λw. the variance σ(X,w) is maximized with w is the eigenvector of Σ, the maximum value is the related eigenvalue λ.

PCA is either done by

  • singular value decomposition of a design matrix;
  • eigenvalue decomposition on the covariance matrix.

Eigenvalue Decomposition

A (non-zero) vector v∈Rn is an eigenvector of a square matrix A∈Rn×Rn if it satisfies the linear equation Av=λv where λ∈R termed the eigenvalue corresponding to v.

Diagonalisable matrice A can be factorized as A=VΛV−1 where Λii​=λi​ and vi​ is the i-th column of V.
